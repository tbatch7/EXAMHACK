Why this works

Not a productivity hack. A scoring model grounded in learning research.

Exam Hack borrows from three boring but useful ideas: past exams reveal patterns, retrieval beats re-reading, and time limits make prioritization matter.

FINDING 01

Past exams show structure

Prior exams reveal recurring topic types, question formats, and marking habits. That is a better signal than guessing from page count alone.

Educational measurement and test design research.

FINDING 02

Retrieval beats re-reading

Testing yourself outperforms passive review because the act of retrieval strengthens memory.

Roediger and Karpicke, Psychological Science, 2006.

FINDING 03

Focus beats uniform coverage

When time is limited, selective effort on high-yield topics is more useful than treating every reading as equal.

Research on desirable difficulties and effortful practice.
